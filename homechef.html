HomeChef

About The Projectc

HomeChef is a responsive web application that gives users the opportunity to save money and make their favorite restaurant-quality dishes at home.

Problem Statement

We must adapt to every change and circumstance in this fast-changing world. Due to the pandemic a huge amount of people, who were enjoying gourmet food at restaurants, are not able to do it anymore for different reasons. They have to cook at home now, missing out on restaurant-quality meals.

Solution

A responsive web app will allow users to create a fantastically delicious restaurant- grade table in their kitchen with simple ingredients, clear directions, and incredible taste. With the HomeChef app, you'll find all the features and options you need to make cooking a breeze and more enjoyable.

My Process

Research

A qualitative analysis was conducted by interviewing 5 people to learn how they choose the recipes they cook, what products they usually use, and how often they cook. After the interview, I was able to understand users' frustrations and needs.

Interview Question

  • How often, in a week, do you cook or would like to cook at home, on avarage?
  • What turns you off about the recipe?
  • What does the sipmle mean to you when cooking a meal?
  • How do you decide on recipe?
  • What feature would you like to see in the cooking app/website? Are you currently using any cooking apps or websites?
  • What makes you decide to come back to a recipe or cooking app/website?

User Frustrations

  • Long recipe descriptions
  • Long preparation
  • Unknown ingredients
  • Difficult language
  • Long cooking time

User Needs

  • Photos or video guide
  • Ability to change portion amount
  • Convertible chart
  • Alternatives to the ingredients list

Ideation

After gathering all the needed information about the users and understanding their main needs, I created a user flow, with four defined keys: read reviews, search with filters, skip to recipe directions and save the recipe to the cookbook.

Wireframes

After conducting user interviews, gathering all the needed information, and understanding what users need, I developed a solution with low-fidelity prototypes. Not everything is beautiful right away. I used the Crazy 8s method for drawing sketches. With paper prototyping, I was able to quickly validate individual design decisions.

As a result of User Testing the following elements were changed:

  • The filter’s categories were grouped in better order, some filters have symbols to present the options - for user’s clarity and easy using
  • The notification was added to the “Save Recipe” action, to notify the user, that recipe was saved and as an option user can be taken to the cookbook
  • Rating area became a button while hovering, to give the user a hint that it can be pressed. The user can leave a review, the “Leave Comment” button was added

Design

Home Screen

The home screen was designed in a way that everything that is most relevant to the user is easily accessible, with a clear interface.

Filter Search

The multi-level search category will allow people who don't have a precise interest in one recipe to find it. And on the other hand, some filters allow you to specify your search.

Recipe Screens

The search results present a huge variety of tasty recipes. Each recipe screen has a short story, helpful information, a short and clear recipe guide with photos, a convertible chart, and access to other viewers' reviews.

Review Screens

Review screens allow the user to read about others' experiences with a particular recipe and see the score of that recipe. Users can also write reviews or comments on already existing ones.

Typography

I chose two fonts for the typography. Montserrat for the titles and headers, which is bold and modern, and Georgia for the body, which is the perfect combination for a simple and modern feel.

Color Palette

In order to keep the portfolio looking modern and simple, I chose three colors for the colour palette.
